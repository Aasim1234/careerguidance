The Big Leap: From Your Medium to English

آپ کے میڈیم سے انگریزی تک کی بڑی چھلانگ

Moving to an English medium for 11th and 12th grade can be challenging, but it's a bridge to a wider world of knowledge. Here are common hurdles and how you can overcome them. This section is here to prepare you, not to scare you. With the right approach, you can excel.

The Challenge: Suddenly, 'Koshika' becomes 'Cell' and 'Gurutvakarshan' becomes 'Gravity'. It feels like learning a new language for every subject.

The Solution:

  • Create a bilingual dictionary for each subject. Write the English term and its Hindi/Urdu equivalent.
  • Use flashcards to memorize new terms daily.
  • Watch educational videos in English on topics you're studying. The visuals will help connect words to concepts.

The Challenge: You understand the teacher, but you're afraid to ask questions or answer them, fearing you'll make a grammatical mistake.

The Solution:

  • Practice speaking English with friends who are supportive. It's okay to make mistakes!
  • Write down your questions before asking. This helps you structure your thoughts.
  • Remember, your teachers want to help you. Your courage to ask is more important than perfect grammar.

The Challenge: Reading textbooks in English takes much longer, and you have to re-read paragraphs to understand them.

The Solution:

  • Start by reading simple English storybooks or news articles for 15-20 minutes daily.
  • Don't look up every single word. Try to understand the meaning from the context of the sentence.
  • Summarize each chapter or major section in your own simple English or even in Hindi/Urdu. This ensures you've understood the core concepts.

Boost Your English Skills

اپنی انگریزی کی مہارتیں بہتر بنائیں

Improving your English can open many doors. Here are some free and easy-to-use resources tailored for Hindi/Urdu speakers to help you along your journey. Consistency is key!

Vocabulary & Grammar

  • Duolingo / Hello English app: Fun and interactive lessons for all levels, with Hindi/Urdu support.
  • Cambridge Dictionary (Online): Provides definitions, examples, and often pronunciation guides.
  • Grammarly (Free version): Helps check your writing for grammatical errors and suggests improvements.
  • "Word of the Day" apps: Build your vocabulary daily with new words and their meanings.

Speaking & Listening

  • YouTube Channels: Search for "Spoken English for Hindi speakers" or "Learn English through Urdu" (e.g., Learn English with Viva, BBC Learning English).
  • TED Talks (with subtitles): Listen to inspiring speeches on various topics and use subtitles initially.
  • English News Channels/Podcasts: Start with slower-paced news or podcasts for learners.
  • Practice Speaking: Find a study partner or use apps like HelloTalk to practice with native speakers.

Reading & Writing

  • Simple English Wikipedia: Read articles on various subjects in simpler English.
  • Short English Storybooks: Start with children's books or simplified novels.
  • Write Daily: Maintain a short diary in English, write emails, or summarize what you've read.
  • News in Slow English: Websites that provide news articles read at a slower pace.
